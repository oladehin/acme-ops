# Quarterly Ops Playbook

## Overview

This playbook provides a structured, repeatable process for planning, executing, and reviewing operations each quarter. The ops team should use it as a living guide — updating it as workflows evolve and new lessons are learned. Each phase below maps to a specific window in the quarter so that nothing slips through the cracks.

## Preparation (Weeks 1-2)

- Pull up last quarter’s retro notes and review every outcome and open action item to determine what was completed, what carried over, and what needs to be deprioritized.
- Check the current budget against upcoming contract renewals, planned purchases, and any outstanding purchase orders to make sure there are no surprises mid-quarter.
- Confirm the staffing plan with hiring managers, verify start dates for incoming new hires, and flag any onboarding tasks that need to be scheduled in advance.

## Execution (Weeks 3-10)

- Monitor the agreed-upon operational metrics on a weekly basis, noting any trends or anomalies that could indicate emerging issues before they escalate.
- Meet regularly with partner teams to surface cross-team dependencies early and ensure that shared milestones stay on track throughout the quarter.
- When blockers arise, escalate them through the appropriate channel within 24 hours and document both the issue and the resolution so the team can reference it later.

## Review (Weeks 11-12)

- Compile the quarter’s key results into a summary document that includes metric performance, completed initiatives, and any goals that were missed along with the reasons why.
- Hold a lessons-learned session with the team to capture what went well, what didn’t, and what specific changes should be made to improve next quarter.
- Archive all important documents, decision records, and supporting artifacts in the shared drive so they remain accessible for future reference and audits.

## Closeout & Carryover (Weeks 13–14)

- Review every open item from the quarter and explicitly decide whether it rolls into the next quarter or gets de-scoped, documenting the rationale for each decision.
- Take the lessons learned from the review phase and translate them into concrete updates to this playbook, such as adding new steps, removing outdated ones, or revising templates.
- Close out all lingering administrative items including purchase orders, outstanding invoices, contract paperwork, and any temporary access grants that should be revoked.
- Finalize and lock the quarter’s artifact set, which should include the final metrics snapshot, the decision log, retrospective notes, and the budget reconciliation.

## Next-Quarter Kickstart (Weeks 15–16)

- Align leadership and the ops team on the top risks, key dependencies, and any milestones that absolutely must not slip in the upcoming quarter.
- Audit the operating metrics to make sure definitions are still accurate, dashboard owners are assigned, and alert thresholds reflect current performance baselines.
- Reconfirm the staffing and onboarding plan with managers, and update on-call rotations and ownership maps to reflect any team changes.
- Publish a short kickoff note to the broader team that outlines the quarter’s goals, important dates, designated owners for each initiative, and how to escalate issues.
